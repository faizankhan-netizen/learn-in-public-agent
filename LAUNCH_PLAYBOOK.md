# X Launch Playbook — Learn in Public

> Your step-by-step guide to launching a "Learn in Public" X account from zero.
> Follow this in order. Don't skip steps. Each one builds on the last.

---

## Phase 0: Pre-Launch Setup (30 minutes)

### Step 1: Choose Your Handle

Your handle is your brand. Choose carefully — changing it later fractures recognition.

**Recommendation: Personal name + action verb**

| Option | Pros | Cons |
|--------|------|------|
| `@khan_builds` | Portable, signals action, memorable | "builds" is common |
| `@khan_learns` | Perfect for Learn in Public brand | Might outgrow "learns" |
| `@khancodes` | Short, techy, clean | Narrow (what if you go beyond code?) |
| `@agentickhan` | Niche-specific, unique, memorable | Locked to the agent niche |
| `@0xkhan` | Hacker aesthetic, very short | Less approachable |

**My top pick: `@khan_builds`** — it's portable (you can build anything), signals action (not passive consumption), and works whether you're building agents, apps, or a business.

> **Action:** Go to x.com/settings and check which handles are available. Pick one and reserve it NOW, even before filling in the rest.

---

### Step 2: Profile Picture

First impression. People decide to follow in 2 seconds.

**Options:**
| Type | When to use |
|------|-------------|
| Clear face photo | Best for personal brand. Trust and connection. |
| Stylized avatar | If you want anonymity. Use a consistent, recognizable avatar. |
| AI-generated portrait | Quick option. Use a tool like Midjourney or a free avatar generator. |

**Rules:**
- High contrast (looks good at 48x48 pixels — that's how it appears in timelines)
- No group photos
- No logos (you're not a company yet)
- Consistent across all platforms

---

### Step 3: Header Image

The banner behind your profile. This is prime real estate.

**What to put there:**
- A simple text banner: "Learning AI in public. Building agents from scratch."
- A screenshot of your code editor / terminal output
- A clean graphic with your content pillars

**Free tools to make it:**
- Canva (free tier) — 1500x500px template
- Or just use a clean dark gradient with white text

---

### Step 4: Write Your Bio

You have 160 characters. Every word must earn its place.

**Formula:** Who you are + What you do + Why follow

**Recommended bio:**

```
learning AI from scratch. building agents, breaking things, sharing everything.
follow along if you're curious about what's possible. 🧪
```
(149 chars)

**Alternative bios:**

```
building AI agents in public. zero expertise, maximum curiosity.
documenting the journey from absolute beginner. 🔨
```
(128 chars)

```
exploring AI agents, open-source LLMs, and automation.
learning by building. sharing the wins and the fails.
```
(112 chars)

**Rules for bios:**
- No "CEO" or "Founder" unless you are one
- No "aspiring" — you ARE doing it
- Lowercase feels more authentic than Title Case
- One emoji max
- Don't list 10 interests — pick 2-3

### Step 5: Location & Website

- **Location:** Your city, or "the internet", or "building in public" — anything that adds personality
- **Website:** Link to your GitHub, your Nexus platform, or leave blank for now

### Step 6: Pinned Tweet (Write This First)

Your pinned tweet is your manifesto. Every profile visitor sees it. It answers: "Why should I care?"

**Write this and pin it immediately:**

```
starting an experiment: learning AI in public.

i know almost nothing about AI agents. 
i'm going to build them, break them, and share everything i learn.

the plan:
→ build a content engine (AI agent that researches and writes)
→ explore open-source LLMs on consumer hardware
→ document every win, fail, and surprise

follow along if you're curious. let's figure this out together. 🧵
```

**Why this works:**
- Honest ("I know almost nothing") — disarms skepticism
- Specific ("content engine", "consumer hardware") — signals real substance
- Inviting ("let's figure this out together") — makes people feel included
- Concrete plan — gives people a reason to stick around

## Phase 1: First 72 Hours (Critical Window)

### Day 1 — Establish Presence

**Morning: Follow 50 people**

Search for and follow accounts in these categories:

| Category | How to find them | How many |
|----------|-----------------|----------|
| AI/ML builders | Search "build AI agent" or "learn in public AI" | 15 |
| Open source maintainers | Check trending repos on GitHub, find their X | 10 |
| Tech educators | Search "explained" + "AI" or "LLM tutorial" | 10 |
| AI newsletter authors | They have the biggest reach in your niche | 5 |
| Other Learn in Public folks | Search #BuildInPublic #LearnInPublic | 10 |

> **Don't follow thousands.** 50 targeted follows on Day 1 is perfect.

**Afternoon: Post your manifesto + first 2 organic tweets**

Tweet 1 (Pinned — already written above)

Tweet 2 — Behind the scenes:
```
day 1 of learning AI in public.

first thing i built: an AI agent from scratch. 
no frameworks. just a while loop that thinks, acts, and observes.

~150 lines of code. it already researches trending AI topics 
and writes tweet drafts for me.

yes, some of what you'll read here was drafted by my own agent. 
i always edit and add my voice though. transparency matters. 🔧
```

Tweet 3 — The hook for tomorrow:
```
tomorrow i'm going to break down how AI agents actually work.

spoiler: it's simpler than you think. 

(and way cooler once you understand the loop)
```

**Evening: Engage with 5-10 existing conversations**

- Find tweets from people you followed
- Reply with something genuine (not "great post!")
- Ask a real question about something they said
- Like and retweet 5-10 things you genuinely find interesting

> **The ratio:** For every 1 tweet you post, engage with 5 other people's content. This is how the algorithm learns you're a human, not a bot.

## Phase 2: Days 4-14 (Build the Habit)

### Daily Routine (15 minutes)

```
Morning (5 min):
  1. Run: npm run agent
  2. Read the briefing
  3. Pick 1-2 tweets, edit them in your voice

Afternoon (5 min):
  4. Post the edited tweets (use X's native scheduler for timing)
  5. Reply to any engagement on yesterday's posts

Evening (5 min):
  6. Engage with 5 other people's tweets in your niche
  7. Like/retweet anything genuinely interesting
```

### Content Mix Per Week

| Type | Frequency | Source |
|------|-----------|--------|
| Agent-generated + edited tweets | 1-2/day | Content Engine output |
| Organic observations/reactions | 2-3/week | Your real experiences |
| Thread (deep dive) | 1/week | Pick a topic you learned that week |
| Reply/engagement | Daily | Other people's tweets |
| Behind-the-scenes | 1-2/week | Screenshots, process, failures |

### Weekly Thread Ideas (First Month)

| Week | Thread Topic |
|------|-------------|
| 1 | How AI agents actually work (the loop) |
| 2 | I built a Content Engine — here's what it does |
| 3 | What open-source LLMs can run on an 8GB laptop |
| 4 | Month 1 review: what worked, what didn't, what's next |

## Engagement Playbook

### How to Reply (Do's and Don'ts)

| ✅ Do | ❌ Don't |
|-------|---------|
| Ask a follow-up question | "Great post!" |
| Share your own experience | "Thanks for sharing!" |
| Respectfully disagree with reasoning | One-word replies |
| Add a specific detail they missed | Tag random people |
| Quote tweet with your take | Immediately pitch yourself |

### Finding Your People

Search these hashtags weekly:
- `#BuildInPublic` — builders sharing their journey
- `#LearnInPublic` — your direct community
- `#LocalLLaMA` — open-source LLM enthusiasts
- `#AIAgents` — your niche
- `#100DaysOfCode` — learners (adjacent audience)

### The Compound Effect

```
Week 1:   5-20 followers (friends, curious people)
Week 2:   20-50 followers (if you're consistent)
Month 1:  50-150 followers (with good engagement)
Month 3:  300-1000 followers (if content is genuinely good)
Month 6:  1000+ followers (compounding kicks in)
```

Don't chase numbers. Chase genuine connections. The numbers follow.

## Red Lines (Things That Kill New Accounts)

1. **Don't buy followers.** Ever. The algorithm knows.
2. **Don't use follow-for-follow.** Attracts bots, not humans.
3. **Don't post identical content across accounts.** X detects this.
4. **Don't automate replies.** Post automation is fine; engagement must be human.
5. **Don't go from 0 to 10 posts/day.** Ramp up gradually.
6. **Don't argue in comments.** Walk away from negativity. You're too new.
7. **Don't delete posts that underperform.** Not everything will hit. That's normal.
